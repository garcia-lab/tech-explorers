üîí Stay Safe Online

Most of these sites are designed for kids, but it's always good to supervise, especially when creating accounts. Look for sites with parent controls.

‚è∞ Set Time Limits

Screen time matters! 30-60 minutes of coding practice a few times a week is better than marathon sessions. Quality over quantity!

üéâ Celebrate Progress

Ask to see what they've built! Showing genuine interest in their creations keeps them motivated to learn more.

ü§ù Code Together

You don't need to be an expert! Learning alongside your child can be a great bonding experience. Try a Code.org course together!

üéØ Start with Interests

Love Minecraft? Try Minecraft Education. Like art? Try Scratch or pixel art coding. Connecting coding to their interests helps motivation.

üì± Mobile Options

Apps like Grasshopper, ScratchJr, and Swift Playgrounds work great on tablets for on-the-go learning!
